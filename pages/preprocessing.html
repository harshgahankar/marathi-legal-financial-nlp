Sentence Segmentation

Dividing text into individual sentences based on punctuation, grammatical cues, and discourse markers.

Creates meaningful linguistic units for downstream tasks like parsing, translation, and information extraction.

Uses punctuation marks (। devanagari danda, ?, !) along with statistical models trained on Marathi sentence boundary patterns.

"मी शाळेत जातो. तो घरी राहतो."
→ ["मी शाळेत जातो.", "तो घरी राहतो."]

Advantages
  • Enables sentence-level analysis
  • Improves translation quality
  • Essential for corpus building
Limitations
  • Ambiguous punctuation
  • Dialogs & quoted speech
  • Noisy web text

Tokenization

Splitting text into smaller units — tokens — typically words, subwords, or characters.

Converts raw text into discrete units that can be mapped to embeddings or vocabulary indices.

Rule-based (whitespace + punctuation splitting) or statistical (BPE, WordPiece, SentencePiece) trained on Marathi corpora.

"मराठी भाषा समृद्ध आहे."
→ ["मराठी", "भाषा", "समृद्ध", "आहे", "."]

Advantages
  • Foundation for all NLP pipelines
  • Subword handles OOV words
  • Flexible granularity
Limitations
  • Large vocab for morph-rich langs
  • Ambiguous compound words
  • Samas (समास) splitting

Stop-word Removal

Filtering out high-frequency words that carry little semantic meaning (postpositions, conjunctions, auxiliary verbs).

Reduces feature dimensionality and focuses the model on content-bearing words.

A curated Marathi stop-word list is matched against tokenized text; matches are discarded unless required by the task.

"मी आणि तो शाळेत गेलो."
→ ["शाळेत", "गेलो"]
(removing "मी", "आणि", "तो")

Advantages
  • Reduces dataset size
  • Improves computational efficiency
  • Boosts classification accuracy
Limitations
  • Risk of removing semantic words
  • Task-dependent relevance
  • Language-specific list needed

Stemming

A heuristic process that chops off affixes to reduce words to a base or root form without full linguistic knowledge.

Normalizes morphological variants so different inflections of the same word map to a single stem.

Rule-based suffix stripping (removing Marathi suffixes like -णे, -तो, -ली, -ले) using predefined patterns.

"खेळणे, खेळतो, खेळली"
→ stem: "खेळ"

Advantages
  • Fast & computationally cheap
  • Improves recall in IR
  • Simple to implement
Limitations
  • Over-/under-stemming
  • Loses morphological info
  • Language-specific rules

Lemmatization

A linguistic process that reduces words to their canonical dictionary form (lemma) using vocabulary and morphological analysis.

Maps all inflected forms to a single lemma while preserving part-of-speech and meaning.

Combines a Marathi morphological analyzer with a lexicon to identify roots by considering POS tags and grammatical context.

"गेलो, गेली, गेले"
→ lemma: "जा" (go)
(gender/number resolution)

Advantages
  • Linguistically valid lemmas
  • Preserves semantic info
  • Superior precision to stemming
Limitations
  • Needs comprehensive lexicon
  • Slower than stemming
  • Weak on OOV words

Noise Removal

Stripping out irrelevant or corrupting elements such as HTML tags, URLs, emojis, special characters, and non-Devanagari script.

Eliminates artifacts that do not contribute to linguistic meaning and could confuse downstream models.

Regex patterns, Unicode range filtering (Devanagari block U+0900–U+097F), and language detection to retain only Marathi-relevant content.

"Check <b>हा</b> article @url 😊"
→ "हा article"

Advantages
  • Prevents model confusion
  • Reduces vocabulary size
  • Improves signal-to-noise ratio
Limitations
  • May strip meaningful symbols
  • Code-mixed text challenges
  • Aggressive removal risk

Text Cleaning

A comprehensive normalization step correcting spelling errors, standardizing numerals, handling ligature normalization, and resolving Unicode inconsistencies.

Ensures textual consistency across documents by fixing orthographic and encoding irregularities common in Marathi digital text.

Applies spelling correction dictionaries, Devanagari numeral mapping (०-९ ↔ 0-9), NFC normalization, and whitespace regularization.

"१९९८" → "1998"
"क्र\u200dम" (with ZWJ) → "क्रम"

Advantages
  • Creates clean standard input
  • Improves model generalization
  • Essential for evaluation
Limitations
  • Needs robust dictionary
  • Over-normalization risk
  • Computationally intensive
