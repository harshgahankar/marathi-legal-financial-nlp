Word Embeddings

Dense, low-dimensional vector representations of words where each word is mapped to a continuous real-valued vector.

Captures semantic and syntactic relationships through geometric proximity — similar words cluster together in vector space.

Learned by training a neural network on large text corpora to predict word co-occurrence patterns; the hidden layer weights become the word vectors.

vec("मुंबई") – vec("महाराष्ट्र")
+ vec("गुजरात")
≈ vec("अहमदाबाद")

Advantages
  • Dense vectors reduce dimensionality
  • Capture analogies via arithmetic
  • Pre-trained embeddings available
Limitations
  • Static — one vector per word
  • Struggles with polysemy
  • Need large corpora for quality

Word2Vec

A predictive neural embedding model that learns word vectors by training on local context windows using CBOW or Skip-gram architectures.

Produces high-quality distributed word representations that capture semantic and syntactic regularities.

CBOW predicts target word from surrounding context; Skip-gram predicts surrounding context from target word. Uses negative sampling or hierarchical softmax for efficiency.

Skip-gram: given "मराठी",
predict ["प्राचीन", "भाषा",
"समृद्ध", "साहित्य"]
(2-word window)

Advantages
  • Efficient on large corpora
  • Captures linear relationships
  • Well-established implementations
Limitations
  • No OOV words (inflectional issue)
  • Ignores subword information
  • Limited long-range dependency

FastText

An extension of Word2Vec that represents each word as a bag of character n-grams, with the final vector as the sum of its subword vectors.

Handles out-of-vocabulary words and morphologically rich languages by leveraging subword information.

Each word is split into character n-grams (3–6 chars); each n-gram gets a vector; the word vector is the sum of all its n-gram vectors. Trained via CBOW or Skip-gram.

"मराठी" → subword n-grams:
"<मर", "मरा", "राठ",
"ाठी", "ठी>", "<मरा" ...
→ vector sum of all n-grams

Advantages
  • Embeds unseen inflected words
  • Ideal for Marathi morphology
  • Fast training
Limitations
  • Higher memory for n-gram storage
  • Noisy vectors for short words
  • Still a static embedding

Contextual Embeddings

Dynamic word representations where each word's vector depends on its surrounding context, generated by deep bidirectional transformer models.

Resolves polysemy and captures contextual nuance — the same word receives different vectors in different contexts.

Transformer models (e.g., BERT, MuRIL, IndicBERT) process the full input sequence through self-attention layers, producing context-aware representations per token.

"बँक" (bank):
"नदीच्या बँकेवर" (bank of river)
→ vector₁ ≠ vector₂ ←
"पैसे बँकेत" (money in bank)

Advantages
  • Handles polysemy perfectly
  • State-of-the-art performance
  • MuRIL/IndicBERT support Marathi
Limitations
  • Computationally expensive
  • GPU needed for inference scale
  • Large model sizes (GB)
